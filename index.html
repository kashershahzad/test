Understanding blood groups is essential for healthcare professionals and individuals alike. Blood is classified into different groups based on the presence or absence of specific antigens on the surface of red blood cells. The two most well-known blood group systems are the ABO system and the Rh system. The ABO system categorizes blood into four main groups A, B, AB, and O. Each group has its unique antigen configuration, and the compatibility of blood transfusions or organ transplants depends on matching these groups. For instance, people with blood type A have A antigens and can receive blood from A and O donors. In contrast, the Rh system adds another layer to this classification, with either a positive (+) or negative (-) designation.
